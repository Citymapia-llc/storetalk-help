The **StoreTalk Zoom App** lets you create Zoom video meetings directly from WhatsApp flows. Customers receive a one-tap join link as a WhatsApp CTA button — no app downloads or meeting codes needed.

## What it does

When a customer triggers a flow that includes the Zoom action, the app creates a Zoom meeting using your credentials and sends a join link to the customer. You can track meeting status, view recordings, and get notified when meetings complete — all automated through StoreTalk.

## Features

- Create Zoom meetings from WhatsApp flow nodes
- One-tap join link sent as a WhatsApp button
- Waiting room support (configurable per tenant)
- Cloud recording with automatic post-meeting delivery
- Meeting history dashboard with status tracking
- Works with BookingPro for online appointment scheduling

## How a typical flow looks

```
Customer sends keyword (e.g. "consult")
        ↓
Flow starts → reaches the Zoom App node
        ↓
Customer taps "Join Meeting" button on WhatsApp
        ↓
Customer joins the Zoom meeting
        ↓
Meeting ends → flow receives completion details
(duration, recording URL available later)
```

## Setting up

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a Zoom Server-to-Server OAuth app">
    Go to [marketplace.zoom.us](https://marketplace.zoom.us), sign in, and create a new **Server-to-Server OAuth** app. Copy the **Account ID**, **Client ID**, and **Client Secret**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install the Zoom app in StoreTalk">
    Go to **Apps → Marketplace**, find **StoreTalk Zoom**, and click **Install**. Give the instance a name (e.g. "Video Consultations").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure your Zoom credentials">
    Click **Open Dashboard** on the app's Dashboard tab. Go to **Settings** and enter your Zoom Account ID, Client ID, and Client Secret. Configure default meeting duration, waiting room, and recording preferences.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a flow">
    In the **Flow Builder**, create a flow with a keyword trigger (e.g. "consult" or "meeting"). Add an **App** node, select your Zoom instance, and set the action to `create_meeting`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish the flow">
    Click **Publish**. Customers can now trigger it by sending the keyword to your WhatsApp number. They'll receive a "Join Meeting" button that opens Zoom directly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Flow variables

The app returns these variables after a meeting completes. Use them in downstream flow nodes with `{{variable_name}}` syntax.

| Variable | Example | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `meeting_id` | `123456789` | Zoom meeting ID |
| `join_url` | `https://zoom.us/j/123...` | Meeting join link |
| `duration_minutes` | `15` | Meeting duration in minutes |

## Using with BookingPro

If you have both **Zoom** and **BookingPro** installed, Zoom can automatically provide meeting links for online appointments:

1. Install both the Zoom app and BookingPro
2. Go to **Settings → Service Defaults** and set Zoom as your default **meet** provider
3. When a customer books an online service in BookingPro, they automatically receive a Zoom join link

<Note>
  If you also have **StoreTalk Meet** installed, you can choose which video provider to use as your default in the Service Defaults settings. BookingPro works with either one.
</Note>

## Meeting lifecycle

| Status | When |
|---|---|
| **Meeting Created** | Zoom meeting created, join link sent to customer |
| **Customer Joined** | Customer clicked the link and entered the meeting |
| **Meeting Completed** | Meeting ended normally |
| **Meeting Abandoned** | Session expired without anyone joining |
| **Recording Available** | Cloud recording is ready (if auto-recording enabled) |

## Pricing

The Zoom app itself is **free**. You need your own Zoom account with a plan that supports the features you need (e.g. cloud recording requires Zoom Pro or higher).

<Tip>
  A free Zoom account works for basic meetings (up to 40 minutes for group calls). For longer meetings or cloud recording, upgrade to Zoom Pro.
</Tip>
